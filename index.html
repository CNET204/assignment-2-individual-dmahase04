The effects of e-waste in the environment

Currently in the world the electrical and electronic waste also known e-waste has tripled. As people today increasingly need electronic devices for communication, entertainment, and productivity, the disposal of outdated or malfunctioning devices has emerged as a pressing environmental concern. E-waste ranges a diverse formation of electronic products, including phones, computers, television, and household tools, which contain hazardous materials such as lead, mercury, and cadmium. Improper handling of e-waste not only creates significant risks to human health and the environment but also valuable resources and exacerbates in the global electronic waste crisis. Against this environment, understanding the landscape of e-waste management becomes paramount, exploring the challenges, initiatives, and opportunities shaping the approach to sustainable electronic waste disposal and recycling.
